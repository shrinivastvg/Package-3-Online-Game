Case Study Scenario [Fictional]

Kancheepuram City Municipal Corporation (KCMC) is improving its water supply system through a project under the Tamil Nadu Climate Resilient Urban Development Program, funded by the World Bank. The project aims to fix old pipelines, lack of overhead tanks, and unreliable pumps. To meet the cityâ€™s water need of 60.36 MLD by 2055, water will be drawn from the Palar River using three advanced collector and infiltration wells at Thirupparkadal.

The project includes laying 450 km of new HDPE pipelines, 33 km of feeder mains, building 14 new overhead tanks (OHTs), and installing automated SCADA systems for efficient water management. It will provide 135 liters of water per person per day to over 55,000 households.

Environmental and social challenges are addressed through a detailed plan to reduce noise, air pollution, and traffic issues during construction while protecting cultural sites and ensuring public safety. This project ensures a reliable water supply, supports climate resilience, and improves the quality of life for residents.
